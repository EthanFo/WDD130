What do I need to know?

One of the first steps to changing is understanding what you’re up against.

**1. Mindful Tech Use: Setting Boundaries In our hyper-connected world, it’s crucial to cultivate a healthier relationship with technology. Rather than mindlessly scrolling through screens, consider implementing screen time boundaries. Allocate specific periods for focused work or leisure without distractions. During meals, put away devices to consciously engage with others and savor your food. By doing so, you’ll harness the power of technology while maintaining meaningful connections and mindful presence

**2. Awareness and Brain Health Scientific research reveals a link between excessive screen time and decreased mental health. Youth who spend significant hours on digital devices are more likely to experience internalizing problems like depression, anxiety, and social anxiety. To counter this, be intentional about your tech use. Prioritize quality interactions over mindless consumption. Consider turning off notifications during driving to prevent distracted accidents. By being aware of the impact of technology on your well-being, you can make informed choices

**3. Intentional Living in the Digital Age Finnian Kelly, an executive coach, emphasizes intentional choice over passive reactivity. To improve your technology habits, try these strategies:

- Educate yourself: Stay informed about tech trends and terminology.

- Use online resources: Problem-solve independently and challenge yourself.

- Talk with experts: Seek guidance when needed.

- Practice daily awareness: Reflect on the benefits and drawbacks of your tech use.

- Remember you’re not alone: Many struggle with balancing technology.

- Don’t give up: Cultivate resilience in creating a healthier tech-life balance.

Remember, technology is a tool—use it intentionally to enhance your life rather than letting it control you.
